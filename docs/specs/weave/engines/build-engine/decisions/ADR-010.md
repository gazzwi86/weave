# ADR-010: Standards-catalogue authz falls back to tenant-admin (no project-admin role exists)

## Status
Accepted (TASK-001, EPIC-002, engineer-authored during implementation)

## Context
TASK-001's brief asks `PUT /api/standards/{scope}/{key}` to gate company-scope
writes on a "company-admin" grant and project-scope writes on a "project-admin"
grant, both "resolved from PLAT-IDENTITY-1 JWT `roles` claim ... same pattern
as the M1 project-settings role check".

Neither half of that instruction matches what the M1 codebase actually has:

- There is no `roles` claim on the verified JWT or on `Principal`
  (`auth/dependencies.py`) anywhere in this codebase. The only role
  infrastructure is `workspace_members.role` (`rbac.py`'s
  `ROLE_RANK`/`enforce_workspace_role`/`is_tenant_admin`), which is a
  workspace-scoped membership table, not a claim.
- The "M1 project-settings role check" (`routers/settings.py`) is
  `_require_own_tenant_scope` (tenant match) + `_require_workspace_role_for_scope`
  (workspace-membership lookup keyed by a `workspace_id` parsed out of a
  `scope_iri`). `projects` (`migrations/0009_projects.sql`) has no
  `workspace_id` column, so there is no way to resolve a "workspace" -- and
  therefore no way to resolve a distinct "project admin" -- from a
  `project_id`/`project_iri` at all.

## Decision
Gate `PUT /api/standards/{scope}/{key}` for **both** `scope=company` and
`scope=project` on the existing `rbac.require_tenant_admin` dependency
(tenant-wide: the caller holds `role='admin'` in at least one
`workspace_members` row for their tenant). No new role table, no new JWT
claim, no workspace-role claim added.

This is deliberately **fail-closed relative to the brief's intent**: tenant
admin is a strict superset of "company admin" or "project admin" as the brief
describes them, so no principal gains write access to the standards catalogue
who wouldn't already have qualified under a narrower check. It does **not**
implement the brief's company vs project 403 distinction -- a principal who is
tenant-admin can write both scopes; there is no separate "project-admin-only,
not company-admin" grant.

`GET /api/standards` and `GET /api/standards/effective` are gated on
`get_current_principal` only (any authenticated tenant member may read) --
matching `routers/settings.py`'s read-side precedent for scopes with no
workspace segment, and consistent with AC-6 (tenant isolation, not role
gating) being the only tested authz property for reads.

## Consequences
- `is_tenant_admin` grants on "admin of any workspace in this tenant", which
  is a slightly broader population than a hypothetical single "company admin"
  concept -- acceptable, over-grant not under-grant, but worth QA's attention.
- If a real per-project admin grant or a `roles` JWT claim lands later (v1.0
  scope per the brief), `PUT /api/standards/{scope}/project/{key}` should be
  revisited to require it instead of tenant-admin, tightening rather than
  loosening.
- No `roles`-claim reader was added to `Principal`/`auth/verify.py` -- out of
  scope for this task, and adding one unilaterally would be new role logic
  the brief explicitly said not to add.

## Alternatives considered
- **Invent a `roles` claim reader now.** Rejected: brief explicitly says "no
  new role logic"; inventing a claim shape without a token issuer that ever
  sets it would be unverifiable and untested.
- **Treat `scope=project` as ungated (401 auth only).** Rejected: fails
  fail-closed -- any authenticated tenant member could write project
  standards, which is worse than the brief's intent, not a safe
  approximation of it.
